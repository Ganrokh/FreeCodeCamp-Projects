Conan O'Brien

The Man. The Comedian. The Pale Legend.

Lonely late night host Conan O'Brien enjoys being away from his family.

    Early Life

  • Conan Christopher O'Brien was born on April 18th, 1963, in Brookline, MA.
  • Father: Dr. Thomas O'Brien,an epidemiologist, the head of microbiology at Peter Brigham Hospital and a professor at Harvard Medical School.
  • Mother: Ruth Reardon O'Brien, a partner at Ropes & Gray Law Firm outside of Boston, MA.
  • Three brothers:
    • Neal, an antique car collector
    • Luke, a lawyer
    • Justin, a business consultant
  • Two sisters:
    • Kate, a teacher
    • Jane, a scriptwriter
  • Attended Harvard University, graduating in 1985 with a bachelor's degree in American History.
  • Twice-elected president of Harvard's parody magazine, The Harvard Lampoon.

    • Early Professional Career

  • Moved to Los Angeles after graduation.
  • Began writing for HBO's Not Necessarily the News.
  • Began performing with the improv group The Groundlings.
  • 1988-1991: Wrote for Saturday Night Live.
    • Won an Emmy for Outstanding Writing in 1989.
  • 1992-1993: Writer and supervisor producer for The Simpsons.
    • Fan favorite episode Marge vs. the Monorail aired on January 14th, 1993.

    • Late Night with Conan O'Brien

  • Began hosting Late Night with Conan O'Brien on September 13th, 1993, taking over from David Letterman.
  • Was only renewed for 13-week intervals for 1993-1997.
  • Finally landed a 5-year contract in 1997.
  • Formed his own television production company, Conaco, in 2001.

    • The Tonight Show

  • Began hosting The Tonight Show on June 1st, 2009, taking over from Jay Leno.
    • Jimmy Fallon took over Late Night.
  • Jay Leno got his own show before Conan's time slot.
  • Jay Leno's new show received poor ratings, hurting Conan's own ratings.
  • NBC tried to move both shows to later timeslots, Conan refused.
  • Conan ended his contract and left the show in January 2010, with Leno taking the show back.

    • Hiatus and Return to Television

  • Conan's exit deal with NBC prevented him from appearing on television until May 2010.
  • He began the Legally Prohibited from Being Funny on Television tour across America on April 12th, 2010, ending on June 14th.
  • He began hosting his new late night talk show, Conan, on November 8, 2010, on TBS.
  • In Fall 2018, Conan announced that his show would be reduced to a 30-minute time slot.
    • He was happy for the change because it allows him to make more YouTube content, more travel shows, and a podcast.
    • Conan's house band of 25 years played their final show on October 4th, 2018.
